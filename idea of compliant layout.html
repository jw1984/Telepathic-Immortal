Important Privacy, Safety & Compliance Notices

General-Audience Software

This software is intended for a general audience and is not designed specifically for children under the age of 13.

Children’s Privacy Notice

This software does not intentionally collect, store, transmit, or process personal information from children under 13.

  • No accounts or profiles
  • No names, emails, or identifiers requested
  • No stored audio or transcripts

If you are under 13, do not enable microphone access. Parents or guardians should discontinue use if they believe a child may have interacted with microphone features.

Microphone & Non-Recording Visualization

This software uses your device microphone only to generate real-time, in-browser visual output.

  • No audio is recorded
  • No audio is stored or saved
  • No audio or derived data is transmitted off-device
  • No sharing, uploading, or retention occurs

Audio signals exist temporarily in memory for visualization and are discarded immediately.

This software is not intended to intercept, monitor, or surveil audio and does not function as a recording tool.

Audio Consent & User Responsibility

Audio consent laws vary by location. Some jurisdictions, including Washington State, require consent from all parties before audio is captured or analyzed.

You are responsible for obtaining all required consent before enabling microphone access in shared or public environments.

Experimental & Educational Use

This project is experimental and intended for educational, artistic, and exploratory purposes only.

It is not a recording device, surveillance system, biometric system, health application, or safety-critical product.

Disclosure Integrity

The descriptions and notices on this page are intended to accurately reflect the software’s actual technical behavior.

No features are intentionally hidden, misrepresented, or enabled without user action.

Security & Third-Party Considerations

Security expectations vary by application scope and distribution. This software minimizes data handling by design.

Browser platforms and third-party libraries may have their own behaviors or limitations. Users should keep systems updated and understand platform-level permissions.
